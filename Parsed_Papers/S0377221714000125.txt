@&#ABSTRACT@&#


               
               
                  Cook and Zhu (2007) introduced an innovative method to deal with flexible measures. Toloo (2009) found a computational problem in their approach and tackled this issue. Amirteimoori and Emrouznejad (2012) claimed that both Cook and Zhu (2007) and Toloo (2009) models overestimate the efficiency. In this response, we prove that their claim is incorrect and there is no overestimate in these approaches.

@&#CONCLUSION@&#


                     Cook and Zhu (2007) and Toloo (2009) dealt with accommodating flexible measures in DEA models. Amirteimoori and Emrouznejad (2012) utilized a numerical example to show that in many real cases Toloo’s revised model is infeasible and then claimed that both models in Cook and Zhu (2007) and Toloo (2009) overestimate the efficiency. In this work, we firstly emphasized that there is a scale of data such that Toloo’s revised model is always feasible. Next, considering all possible combinations of flexible measures, we proved that there is no overestimate in Cook and Zhu (2007) and Toloo (2009) approaches.
